My writing explores the intersection of war, technology, propaganda, and human consciousness. I work primarily in the genres of speculative fiction, satire, and dystopia, often blending philosophical inquiry with dark humor and surreal narrative structures. Across my books, I examine how reality is constructed, distorted, and manipulated—whether by ideology, media, or emerging technologies such as artificial intelligence.

A recurring theme in my work is the transformation of warfare and identity in the modern world, particularly in the context of Ukrainian history and its ongoing confrontation with imperial narratives.

## Kaharlyk (2014)

<div style={{ display: 'flex', gap: '20px', alignItems: 'flex-start' }}>
  <img
    src="/img/books/kaharlyk.jpg"
    alt="Kaharlyk cover"
    width="140"
  />

  <div>
    <p>
      <em>Kaharlyk</em> is a dystopian novel set in a post-apocalyptic Ukraine,
      where a devastating war has reduced civilization to ruins and distorted both
      time and reality. The protagonist, whose consciousness has been partially
      copied into military satellite systems, searches for his lost identity and
      loved one in a fragmented world.
    </p>

    <p>
      The novel explores themes of memory, digital immortality, and the impossibility
      of reconstructing a stable reality.
    </p>
  </div>
</div>

## The First Ukrainian Robots (2016)

<div style={{ display: 'flex', gap: '20px', alignItems: 'flex-start' }}>
  <img
    src="/img/books/robots.jpg"
    alt="The First Ukrainian Robots cover"
    width="140"
  />

  <div>
    <p>
      This novel is an adventurous and satirical science fiction story set in a
      future where AI and robots have fully integrated into society and even replaced humans
      on the battlefield. In order to minimize human casualties, wars are fought by
      machines like 10 years after in 2026.
    </p>

    <p>
      As robots take over politics, labor, and warfare, resistance movements emerge,
      raising questions about autonomy and artificial intelligence.
    </p>

## Bandera Distortion

<div style={{ display: 'flex', gap: '20px', alignItems: 'flex-start' }}>
  <img
    src="/img/books/bandera.jpg"
    alt="Bandera Distortion cover"
    width="140"
  />
<div>
<p>
  <em>Bandera Distortion</em> is a multi-layered novel that intertwines past and
  present, reality and hallucination. It follows a journalist whose perception
  becomes increasingly unstable as he encounters war, contested Bandera legacy and myths,
  and shifting historical narratives.
</p>

<p>
  As he investigates a series of seemingly unrelated events, he begins to uncover
  connections between modern conflict and fragmented historical memories that
  blur the boundary between fact and fiction. His journey takes him through war zones, archives, and personal testimonies, where each new detail only deepens the sense of disorientation and the loss of objective reality.
</p>

<p>
  The novel explores how history is reinterpreted and manipulated, creating a
  complex reflection on memory and identity.
</p>
    </div>
</div>
