# My PhD: Exploring the Development of Ukrainian Science Fiction

> **Focus:** Ukrainian science fiction as a reflection of political, cultural, and technological change.

The genre of Ukrainian science fiction (SF) has undergone significant transformations, shaped by the political, cultural, and technological shifts of each era. From its origins in the early 20th century to its contemporary manifestations, Ukrainian SF reflects a complex interplay of ideology, mysticism, and societal aspirations.

:::tip 
Research Goal
My PhD research explores how Ukrainian science fiction mirrors and responds to changing historical conditions and evolving national identity.
:::

---

## The Foundations of Ukrainian Science Fiction

Although fantastical elements in Ukrainian literature can be traced as far back as *The Paterik of the Kievan Caves Monastery* (13th century), these works do not qualify as science fiction.

:::info 
Key Concept: Positivism
Science fiction emerged as a response to **positivism** — the belief that scientific and technological progress is the main path to human improvement.
:::

In Ukrainian SF, this philosophy became prominent in the 1920s, during rapid industrialization and utopian socialist optimism.

- Sandro Kasyanyuk’s *Machining Humanity* depicts harmony between humans and machines  
- Technology is presented as a tool for building a socialist future  

:::warning 
Early Ukrainian SF was deeply influenced by **Soviet propaganda**, which restricted creative freedom and imposed ideological narratives.
:::

---

## From Naive Optimism to Ideological Disillusionment

The 1920s vision of **techno-communism** combined:

- Marxist materialism  
- belief in technological progress  
- utopian social engineering  

Examples include:

- *Solar Machine* — Volodymyr Vynnychenko  
- *Argonauts of the Universe* — Volodymyr Vladko  

:::tip 
Interpretation
These works suggest that technology could empower the proletariat and eliminate class divisions.
:::

Sandro Kasyanyuk’s works provide a more experimental version of this idea:

- *First Steps* → human-machine fusion  
- *New Utopia* → total technological abundance  

---

### Shift in the 1960s

:::danger 
Turning Point
The 1960s marked a break from rigid Soviet ideology.
:::

Writers began exploring:

- mysticism  
- existential philosophy  
- alternative futures  

Oles Berdnyk emerged as a key figure, blending science fiction with spirituality and challenging official ideology — which led to his imprisonment.

---

## The Golden Age of Ukrainian Science Fiction

The 1970s–1980s represent a **Golden Age**:

:::tip 
Key Features
- philosophical depth  
- reduced ideological pressure  
- experimentation with identity and consciousness  
:::

Notable works:

- *Self-Discovery* — Volodymyr Savchenko  
- *The Guest*, *The Last Signal* — Ihor Rosokhovatskyi  

:::info 
Emerging Theme
AI, cloning, and identity become central concerns — anticipating later global SF trends.
:::

Additional contributors:

- Yevhen Filimonov → psychological effects of technology  
- Viktor Polozhiy → memory, perception, identity  

---

## Postmodernism and the 1990s Crisis

:::warning 
Historical Context
The collapse of the USSR created:
- economic instability  
- identity crisis  
- cultural fragmentation  
:::

Key developments:

- shift toward Russian-language publishing  
- rise of postmodern irony and satire  

Writers like Boris Stern reflected societal collapse through fragmented narratives.

---

### Cultural Resistance

:::tip 
Important Exception
Halyna Pahutyak resisted re-Russification.
:::

Her novel *The Servant from Dobromyl*:

- draws on Ukrainian folklore  
- critiques Soviet imperialism  
- reinforces cultural identity  

---

## The Metamodern Revolution of the 2010s

:::info 
New Phase: Metamodernism
A synthesis of:
- irony (postmodernism)  
- sincerity (modernism)  
:::

Contemporary Ukrainian SF integrates:

- Western sci-fi traditions  
- local historical and political themes  

Examples:

- *Lazarus* — Svetlana Taratorina  
- *The Fifth Park* — Maxim Gah  

:::tip 
Key Insight
Modern Ukrainian SF blends cyberpunk, folklore, and political critique into hybrid narrative forms.
:::

---

## The Importance of My Research

:::tip 
Core Contribution
My research shows how science fiction functions as a **mirror of national identity and historical transformation**.
:::

Ukrainian SF has evolved through:

1. techno-utopian optimism  
2. ideological control  
3. philosophical exploration  
4. postmodern fragmentation  
5. metamodern synthesis  

:::info 
Final Insight
Science fiction is not just literature — it is a tool for understanding how societies imagine their future under pressure.
:::

Through this work, I aim to position Ukrainian science fiction within the broader global tradition and highlight its unique intellectual and cultural contributions.
